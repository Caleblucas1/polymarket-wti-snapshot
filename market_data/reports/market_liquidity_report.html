Use the buttons above the chart to compare effective dollars, raw five-point dollars, and raw five-point shares. Effective depth gives each order half as much weight for every probability point it sits away from the best quote. At very low probabilities, a huge share count can represent modest dollar value; if the best bid is below five cents, this band can also include almost the full bid book.

Lower bars are easier to push: the metric is the smaller of exponentially distance-weighted bid and ask dollar depth. A one-sided book has no displayed resistance in one direction. This is an order-book estimate, not a guarantee against cancellations or hidden liquidity.

Stronger displayed prices sit toward the upper-left: narrower spreads and more executable liquidity on the weaker side. Bubble size reflects current-listing volume.

Session comparisons are preliminary. At least 8–12 snapshots spanning multiple sessions are needed before treating the pattern as evidence.
